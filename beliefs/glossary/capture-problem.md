# capture problem

Analyzed by Jonathan Grudin for groupware generally and fatal to the
[design-rationale](/beliefs/glossary/design-rationale.md) tools of the
1980s–90s regardless of how good their representations were. The enduring
lesson is that rationale capture succeeds only as a **byproduct of the work
itself**, never as an extra authoring task. An agent-operated system inverts
the economics — the agent bears the authoring cost, at decision time, inside a
motion it performs anyway — which is why the old representation research
becomes newly applicable (elaborated in the filed
[design-rationale concept](/knowledge/knowledge-management/design-rationale/design-rationale.md)).
