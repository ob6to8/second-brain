# fitness for purpose

A thing is *fit for purpose* when it is measured against the job it exists to do,
rather than against a single uniform ideal. The consequence that matters for
design: two components serving different functions can be *correct* while
optimizing for opposite properties — what is a virtue in one is a defect in the
other.

**In this brain:** the direction behind
[fit each layer to its purpose](/meta/doctrine/fit-each-layer-to-its-purpose.md) —
the knowledge layer's purpose (understanding on demand) makes concision +
queryability its fitness, while the provenance layer's purpose (reconstructing
what was decided and why) makes fidelity its fitness, so distilling and keeping
verbatim are each right for their own layer rather than competing on one axis.
