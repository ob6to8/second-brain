# is–ought gap

Hume's observation that a conclusion about what *ought* to be cannot be deduced
from premises solely about what *is* — the descriptive and the normative are
different registers, and no purely factual set entails a value. A
[normative conclusion](/beliefs/glossary/normative-conclusion.md) is therefore
never *forced* by a [descriptive premise](/beliefs/glossary/descriptive-premise.md);
it can only be *grounded* by one — the fact plus what a system is *for* makes a
direction reasonable without logically compelling it.

**In this brain:** the gap is the reason "formalize a slogan" is a deliberate act
rather than a derivation — the [is-to-ought analysis](/meta/analysis/is-to-ought-belief-grounds-doctrine.md)
lifts a normative conclusion into a [doctrine](/beliefs/glossary/doctrine.md) and
keeps the descriptive premises beside it as grounding, never pretending the one
entails the other.
