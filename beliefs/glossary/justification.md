# justification

The recorded license for holding a belief. The term carries distinct senses in
different traditions:

1. **TMS** — the record of one derivation step: the set of nodes that jointly
   license a conclusion (Doyle's SL-justification with its inlist/outlist). A
   belief stays held while at least one of its justifications is valid; this
   is the second node class in a [bipartite](/beliefs/glossary/bipartite-graph.md)
   belief/justification graph.
2. **GSN** — a *rationale annotation*: an oval-'J' element presenting why a
   goal or strategy is appropriate. In GSN the inferential role is played by
   the *strategy*, not the justification.
3. **Toulmin / general epistemology** — the warranted support offered for a
   claim; justification-first ("here is why I hold this") as opposed to
   inference-first reasoning.

Sense collisions between (1) and (2) are easy to trip over when reading across
the TMS and assurance-case literatures.
