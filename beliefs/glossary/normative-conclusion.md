# normative conclusion

A claim in the *ought* register: it says what should be done, valued, or aimed
for, rather than what is the case. Unlike a [descriptive premise](/beliefs/glossary/descriptive-premise.md),
it has no truth value — it is adhered-to or abandoned, wise or misguided — and no
set of facts alone entails one (the is–ought gap). A normative conclusion is
*grounded* when a descriptive premise, together with what a system is for, makes
adopting it the reasonable move; the `is → ought` step is the bridge.

**In this brain:** normative conclusions live at two altitudes — a
[doctrine](/beliefs/glossary/doctrine.md) states one as a general standing
direction, and a [policy](/beliefs/glossary/policy-type.md) implements it as a
concrete, enforceable rule. Formalizing a worldview slogan is the act of lifting
its normative conclusion into a doctrine while keeping the descriptive premises
that ground it; the structure is worked through in the
[is-to-ought analysis](/meta/analysis/is-to-ought-belief-grounds-doctrine.md).
