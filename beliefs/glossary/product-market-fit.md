# product-market fit

Commonly tested with heuristics rather than a single metric: the Sean Ellis test (asking active users how disappointed they'd be if the product disappeared) and the effort test (whether the founder must keep pushing users to stay engaged, versus usage sustaining itself). Early traction — a launch spike, friendly early adopters, a viral post — is not the same signal, since it reflects transient forces rather than durable demand; genuine product-market fit is a pattern that holds across multiple cycles, not a single data point.
