# software factory

Two senses share the string. The 2000s sense (Microsoft's "software factories")
meant industrialized reuse: product lines, templates, and code generation
operated by human engineers. The 2026 sense — fixed by StrongDM's internal
experiment and Simon Willison's write-up of it — is stronger: a pipeline whose
rules forbid humans from writing *or reviewing* code, verified by
[holdout scenarios](/beliefs/glossary/holdout-scenario.md) and
[satisfaction metrics](/beliefs/glossary/satisfaction-metric.md) instead of
review. Taken to its lights-out limit, a software factory is the software
instance of a [dark factory](/beliefs/glossary/dark-factory.md).
