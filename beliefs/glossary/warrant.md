# warrant

In the Toulmin model, the statement *authorizing the movement* from grounds to
claim: the license — usually left implicit in real argument — that makes these
facts count as support for this conclusion, itself certified by *backing* when
challenged. The warrant is what distinguishes a mere co-occurrence of premises
and conclusion from a genuine inference step, which is why it attaches
naturally to the [justification](/beliefs/glossary/justification.md) node of a belief
graph and is the thing a per-edge LLM entailment judgment actually evaluates.
GSN's *strategy* element is its engineering descendant.
